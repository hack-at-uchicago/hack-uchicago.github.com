Hackathon

On February 10-11 we’ll be holding our very first Hackathon, open to hackers across the University. The theme, broadly, is improving the quality of life at UChicago, but we’ll accepting other entries. Hackers may arrive on the 10th with ideas and teams, but we’ll also facilitate the formation of new teams with pitches and introductions. If this sounds interesting, make sure to register with the event on Facebook, where you can also find ideas and hackers to work with. There will also be a $100 prize going to the best project, to be judged by peers.

Hack Nights

Hack Nights are a time set aside each week for coders and anyone generally interested in technology to gather and hack. We meet every Friday evening from 4:30 to around 8:00 PM in Ryerson 255 (the big lounge), and we usually end up ordering pizza and doing demos around 5 or 6.

Anyone is free to work on projects or homework, mingle with fellow students, and even just hang out. Beginners and experts alike are welcome to join in—there will be experienced students in the room specifically to help beginners learn more about coding and provide pointers on projects.

Get in Touch

If you just want to chat, we’re pretty active on the hack@uchicago Facebook group. We also admister two UChicago listhosts, hack-announce and hack-discuss, and our very own IRC server—connect to willow.uchicago.edu? and tune in to #hackers.
